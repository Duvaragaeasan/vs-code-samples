Brihadisvara Temple

In 1010 AD, Raja Raja built the Brihadisvara Temple in Thanjavur dedicated to Lord Shiva. the temple and the Capital acted as a center of both religious and economic activity. It is also known as Periya Kovil, RajaRajeswara Temple and Rajeswaram. It is one of the largest temples in india and is an example od Dravidian architecture during the Chola period. The template turned 1000 years old in 2010. The temple is part of the UNESCO

World Heritage Site known as the Great Living Chola Temple, with the other two being the Gangaikonda Cholapuram and Airavatesvara temple.
